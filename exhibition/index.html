Limits Of Pixels: Conflict, Control, and Systems!

Exhibition curated by Jonathan Castillo

Curatorial Statement

This exhibition is about how pixels, even though they are really small, can turn into spaces of conflict and competition online. In r/place, pixels become something people fight over, since different communities are trying to build their own images but also defend them from others. I was part of one of these groups, “arkeanos logo” and it showed how people had to work together but also deal with others trying to mess it up. Then in “The Million Dollar Homepage”, pixels are not fought over directly but instead bought and sold, which creates a different type of competition where people are trying to get attention and control space through money. It still feels like a kind of conflict but in a more economic way. Finally, “Every Icon” shows something different, where the conflict is not between people but inside the system itself. The program tries to generate every possible image on a 32x32 grid, but it becomes way too much and basically impossible to finish. All three of these show that pixels are not just simple parts of an image but can become places where different kinds of tension and struggle happen.
